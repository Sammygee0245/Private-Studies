Q1. What is a feeding trial

A feeding trial is a type of scientific study in which the effects of feeding a particular diet to a group of animals or human subjects is observed and recorded. The purpose of a feeding trial is to determine the nutritional value, safety, and efficacy of a particular food or ingredient. The trial typically involves measuring various physiological and health-related parameters, such as weight, blood lipid levels, glucose levels, and nutrient absorption, before and after the feeding period.
